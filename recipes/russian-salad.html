Russian Salad (Olivier Salad)

Description

Often called Olivier Salad (Салат Оливье), this is arguably the most iconic salad in Russia and many former Soviet countries. It's a staple at holiday celebrations and family gatherings. This classic version features a creamy mix of diced boiled vegetables, eggs, pickles, and often a protein like chicken or bologna, all bound together with mayonnaise.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 medium Potatoes
  • 2-3 medium Carrots
  • 4-5 large Eggs
  • 1 cup canned Green Peas, drained
  • 4-5 medium Dill Pickles (or Gherkins), finely chopped
  • Optional: 1 cup cooked Chicken breast or Ham/Bologna, diced
  • 1/2 small Onion, finely chopped (optional, adds sharpness)
  • Approx. 1 cup Mayonnaise (adjust to desired creaminess)
  • Salt and freshly ground Black Pepper to taste
  • Fresh Dill or Parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook Vegetables & Eggs: Boil potatoes and carrots (in their skins) until tender but still firm. Boil eggs until hard-cooked. Let everything cool completely.
  2. Dice Ingredients: Peel the cooled potatoes, carrots, and eggs. Dice them into small, uniform cubes (about 1/4 inch or 0.5 cm). Finely chop the dill pickles and onion (if using). If adding meat, dice it to a similar size.
  3. Combine: In a large bowl, combine the diced potatoes, carrots, eggs, pickles, green peas, onion (if using), and meat (if using).
  4. Dress the Salad: Add mayonnaise gradually, mixing gently until all ingredients are coated to your desired consistency. Be careful not to mash the vegetables.
  5. Season: Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Mix again gently.
  6. Chill: For best flavor, cover the salad and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours (or overnight) to allow the flavors to meld.
  7. Serve: Stir gently before serving. Garnish with fresh dill or parsley if desired. Serve chilled as a side dish or appetizer.
